--- layout: project title: Countach 3D slug: Countach3d company: Personal Project genre: 3D Art role: 3D Artist tech: Maya image_count: 9 --- A full vehicle model of the Lamborghini Countach, built in two weeks.

Breakdown

This came out of a communications class where the assignment was to explain something you're interested in to people who know nothing about it. I figured it'd be a good excuse to break down how 3D modeling actually works.

I'd tried modeling cars before but never actually finished one, there's always another detail to add. This time I gave myself a hard two-week deadline with no extensions, which changed how I approached everything. I had to ask myself what actually matters, what reads from a distance, and what's invisible unless you're zoomed in way too far. Since my audience wasn't artists, I could get away with a lot more.

The Countach made sense as a subject because those sharp angles and that geometric silhouette are so iconic.

I can still see all the imperfections when I look at it, like the blurred glass hiding the fact that there's no interior because I ran out of time. But from a normal viewing distance it reads as a Countach, and that's what matters.

Key Accomplishments

  • Modeled complete vehicle from reference images
  • Built efficient topology for hard surface automotive forms
  • Delivered final presentation within two-week deadline
